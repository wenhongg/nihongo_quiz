## Grammar to Practice Next

### Priority 1: Solidify Current Grammar

You've been working heavily on passive and causative-passive. These are among the hardest N4 grammar points, so keep drilling them.

**Passive (受身形)** — You're getting comfortable. Try using it more naturally in conversation rather than just Q&A drills. Challenge yourself to tell a short story using passive voice.

**Causative-Passive (使役受身形)** — Still fresh. Practice conjugating more verbs:

| Dictionary | Causative-Passive | Meaning |
|-----------|-------------------|---------|
| 食べる | 食べさせられる | to be made to eat |
| 飲む | 飲まされる | to be made to drink |
| 走る | 走らされる | to be made to run |
| 読む | 読まされる | to be made to read |
| 待つ | 待たされる | to be made to wait |

### Priority 2: New Grammar Points to Introduce

**～てもらう / ～てくれる / ～てあげる (Giving & receiving favors)**
- You haven't covered these yet and they're essential N4 grammar
- Example: 友達に日本語を教えてもらった — "My friend taught me Japanese (and I'm grateful)"

**～そうだ (Looks like / I heard that)**
- Two forms: appearance (美味しそう — "looks delicious") and hearsay (雨が降るそうだ — "I heard it's going to rain")

**～ようにする vs ～ことにする**
- You've used ことにする. Compare it with ようにする (making an effort/habit)
- 毎日運動するようにしています vs 運動しないことにしています

**～のに (Despite / even though)**
- 勉強したのに、テストに落ちた — "Despite studying, I failed the test"

### Priority 3: Stretch Goals (Late N4 / Early N3)

**～ば conditional**
- You've practiced ～たら and ～ても. Add ～ば for a complete conditional toolkit
- 安ければ買います — "If it's cheap, I'll buy it"

**Potential form review**
- 見つけられましたか came up in your lesson — make sure all potential conjugations are solid
